An Aid to Building Your Website

1.Know Your Brand

Initially, it's essential to acquaint yourself with your brand and the audience you aim to reach. Crafting a mood board can be a beneficial initial step in this process. The objective is to delve into your core values and transform them into a visual narrative, gathering pertinent assets and elements that embody these values throughout the journey.

BT Games illustrating their brand values.

Integrating your brand's values and mission into your style guide is a strategic move, providing a solid reference point for consistency and coherence in design decisions.

2.Determine Your Logo Usage Rules

Moving forward, it's crucial to establish guidelines for utilizing your logo across its various iterations. If your team includes a graphic or brand designer, it's advisable to collaborate with them to explore existing guidelines.

These may encompass aspects such as the logo's grid, typography, color palette, precise spacing and positioning, visibility across different backgrounds, and other pertinent considerations.

Nike's logo usage guidelines.

Furthermore, ensure to incorporate guidelines outlining what should and shouldn't be done regarding the usage of the logo by third parties, including affiliates.

3.Define Your Color Palette

Color holds significant influence over the perception and memorability of both your website and brand. As such, it's imperative to define your core primary colors, typically limited to a maximum of three.

Additionally, to round out your color palette comprehensively, it's essential to incorporate secondary, tertiary, and neutral colors.

4.Typography

Similar to color, typography holds significant importance in web design. Establishing an appealing and consistent typographic system across your website requires the creation of a typography hierarchy within your style guide.

Begin by outlining the font families and sizes designated for your primary headings, ranging from H1 to H6. This hierarchy sets the foundation for a cohesive typographic identity across your digital platform.

Typography hierarchy by Mailchimp.

Beyond headings, it's essential to incorporate styles for running text, forms, alert messages, and other elements within your design system. These styles encompass font families, which consist of sets like Roboto, Lato, or Montserrat, along with their respective sizes. This ensures consistency and coherence across various textual components throughout your website.

Font sizes in your design should be defined using pixels (px) to ensure consistent rendering across various devices and browsers. Consistency is key across different elements of your design. Standard sizes such as 14px, 16px, 18px, or 20px are recommended for their widespread acceptance and ease of implementation.

By adhering to these standard sizes, you can maintain a cohesive visual hierarchy and streamline the development process. Avoiding intermediate sizes, such as 15px, helps to simplify decision-making and reduces the risk of inconsistent rendering or spacing issues across different components of your design. This approach contributes to a more polished and professional appearance while facilitating a smoother user experience on your website.
